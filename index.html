Where Walls Fall

Restrictions in the guise of equality, walls raised in the name of protection. Policies dressed as defense that decide who moves "freely" and those trapped darkly by the gate.
Division is the child of hate; hate is born of the fear we create. Fear drawn from tales passed around like syphilis, sore from lies born in bitterness.
Red lines the streets of opportunity, corridors narrowing with every stroke. Jealousy burns the city of success, a quiet fire, buried in cinders, smoke settling on dreams broken and torn.
A pool, buried in a park, water drained rather than shared. A Confederate flag, torn apart, finally yielding to the weight of history, the true purpose, hate, was never a mystery.
They carved out "race" from smoke and air, a fiction built to sort and rank. "My neighbor is my enemy." A tale of difference, sharp and deep, so those in power, power keep.
But boundaries are brittle when held up to the light. They crack at the touch of a truth long known but spoken in whispers.
Voices once muted rise in a hum, then a chorus. Change rarely shouts at first; it gathers, slow breath, soft step, until silence can no longer hold.

— Kimberly Williams

"Systemic Metamorphosis" - This piece reflects the evolving forms of systemic racism in the United States, from slavery to the Jim Crow era to modern mass incarceration. The butterfly, seemingly beautiful at first glance, is marked by crow feathers that evoke Jim Crow and the failures of Reconstruction. The chains reveal the ongoing reality of systemic oppression, particularly through mass incarceration. The work also highlights the irony of punitive immigration policies. Walls built to keep people out ultimately confine immigrants within harm’s reach and inflict damage on both immigrant communities and U.S.-born citizens.

— Avery Wolfenbarger
